History

356
The Porsche 356 is a car that holds a special place in automotive history. As the first production automobile by Porsche, it set the stage for the brand's legacy of engineering excellence, performance, and timeless design.
"1948: The first Porsche sports car is introduced."
911
In 1963, Porsche introduced the 911, a car that would become one of the most iconic and enduring sports cars in automotive history. Originally unveiled at the Frankfurt Motor Show, the 911 replaced the Porsche 356 and set new standards for design, performance, and engineering.
Introduction of the Iconic Porsche 911
Cayenne
In 2002, Porsche made a bold move by entering the SUV market with the introduction of the Cayenne. This marked a significant departure from the brand’s traditional lineup of sports cars, aiming to combine Porsche’s high-performance standards with the versatility and practicality of an SUV.
2002: Launch of the first Porsche SUV
Taycan
In 2020, Porsche made a significant leap into the electric vehicle (EV) market with the introduction of the Taycan. As Porsche’s first all-electric sports car, the Taycan combined cutting-edge technology with the brand’s renowned performance and driving dynamics, setting a new benchmark for electric vehicles.
Debut of Porsche’s first electric sports car.

Ruling over Generations

The Porsche 911 is one of the greatest sports cars ever built and the most successful car made by Porsche. Can you imagine it to be called anything else but 911? We neither but the fact is, it was originally named 901 and it had to be changed because a French automotive, Peugeot had a trademark on the “digit-zero-digit” model name. So, the Porsche was renamed to 911. In fact, 82 units of were made Porsche 901 for testing/exhibitions but never sold any of the original models.
